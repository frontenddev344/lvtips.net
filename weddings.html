Top Wedding Venues in Las Vegas

Eiffel Tower at Paris Las Vegas

Get married 460 feet above the Strip on the observation deck of this iconic landmark. With stunning views and French cuisine, it's a romantic spot for a memorable ceremony.

The Neon Museum

For a vintage Vegas vibe, this museum offers a unique setting among historic neon signs and the famed Boneyard. It's perfect for a retro glam wedding.

The Little Vegas Chapel

Located in the Downtown Arts District, this venue offers traditional ceremonies, Elvis-themed weddings, and everything in between. It's an all- inclusive option with beautiful chapels and helpful coordinators.

Chapel of the Flowers

A classic choice with nearly 60 years of experience, offering indoor chapels, rustic gardens, and all-inclusive packages. They ensure an elegant and enchanting wedding experience.

A Special Memory Wedding Chapel

Known for its variety, including drive-thru weddings and outdoor gazebos. It offers comprehensive packages and doesn't charge extra for up to 100 guests​

Getting married in Las Vegas is a thrilling and effortless experience, and our website, LVTips, is here to guide you through it. To kick off your Las Vegas wedding adventure, start by obtaining a marriage license. Head to the Clark County Marriage License Bureau, conveniently located on the Las Vegas Strip. The bureau is open every day of the week, including weekends and holidays, so you can fit this task into your busy schedule. Make sure to bring valid identification such as a driver’s license, passport, or state ID. If either partner has been previously married, you'll also need to provide proof of divorce or a death certificate.

With your marriage license in hand, it’s time to choose the perfect venue. Las Vegas offers a dazzling array of options, from elegant chapels to scenic outdoor settings. Popular choices include iconic wedding chapels along the Strip, which cater to everything from traditional ceremonies to unique themed experiences, including those with Elvis impersonators. For a more luxurious touch, consider a high-end hotel or a picturesque rooftop or garden setting.

Many venues and chapels offer customizable wedding packages that can include floral arrangements, photography, and video services. While most locations have on-site officiants who can perform your ceremony, you also have the option to bring your own officiant if you have specific preferences.

After your ceremony, your officiant will take care of the paperwork and submit it to the county. You’ll then receive your marriage certificate, which can be picked up in person or ordered online. This certificate is essential for any legal matters, such as name changes or spousal benefits.
